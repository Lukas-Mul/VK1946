7,099,411 voters took part in the elections in Czechoslovakia.


The Communist Party of Czechoslovakia was defeated in Slovakia, but in the whole country it was victorious. In total, it won 43.3% of the vote.


Land redistribution, a positive perception of the Soviet Union, and resulting expectations of a new, more socially just system, played a major role.


The National Socialists finished second with 25.2% of the vote. They were
the opposite of the Communists, but their programme probably failed to resonate in rural areas.


The People’s Party hoped for a higher number of voters in rural areas, but it had
to make do with only 16.3% of the vote. This was the beginning of the party’s internal problems.


Social Democrats got only 15% of the vote despite having a very similar programme
to the Communists.


The 1946 elections were the last free democratic elections in the next forty years.
